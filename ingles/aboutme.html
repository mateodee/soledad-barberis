My story

My name is Soledad Barberis, but I prefer people calling me Sol. I have always felt attracted by art and its different forms, leaning more towards painting and scuplture. Not long ago, I introduced myself into the world of collage. At first I thought that the fact I don't have any knowledge on the use of design programs such us Photoshop would be an impediment, but instead the constant use of the scissors let me improve my fine motor skills and it also took me to a special place in my childhood I had once forgotten. I also found a way to escape the everyday routine and the pleasure of doing something I really enjoy and, that brings me peace. There are days when I can't wait to get inside this world of scissors and paper and let time pass without even noticing it, and meet my true self there. Through the images I use, their colors, textures, characters and objects, I try to express my thoughts and feelings.

Studies

In the 90's I moved to Paris for five years, where I studied History of Art at the Louvre School and learned french. Back in Argentina I started at a job that has nothing to do with art, but I always felt the need to be doing any activity that connected me with my artistic side. So I did a year of drawing classes with Sofía Huidobro and two years of Interior Design at the Escuela Argentina de Mujeres.
I took classes on Art History at the National Museum of Bellas Artes for two years. Also, I did some workshops on sublimation with Araceli Pourcel, on creative unblocking at Espacio Dos Puntos and in 2020, during the pandemic,I did two online workshops on Experimental Collage (Levels I and II) with Agustín Galickas, at Taller Rogríguez.

Technique

I feel really attracted to images with bright colors, with flowers and nature, and human figure too. I really admire photographs, paintings, nice textures and especially good aesthetic. My work is inspired by both artists and the wonderful nature that surrounds us, intervening images to create my own interpreation. Doing collage takes me to a place deep inside me, almost like a dream, a place I go everytime I need to find me again.
